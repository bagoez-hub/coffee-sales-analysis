# Coffee Sales Summary Report

This summary is based on the visual outputs in `reports/figures` and highlights the main revenue and product behavior patterns.

## Key Findings

### 1) Revenue by Hour

- Revenue is heavily concentrated in the core daytime window.
- The strongest hourly peak appears at **10:00** (highest bar).
- A secondary high point appears at **16:00**.
- Very early (**06:00-07:00**) and late evening (**22:00**) hours are clearly the weakest.
- From roughly **08:00 to 19:00**, revenue stays consistently strong compared to opening and closing hours.

Operational implication: prioritize staffing and service speed around the late-morning and mid-afternoon peaks, while using opening/closing hours for prep and cleanup-heavy tasks.

### 2) Revenue by Weekday

- **Tuesday** shows the highest weekday revenue.
- **Monday** and **Friday** are also strong performers.
- **Sunday** is the weakest day.
- Weekend performance is lower than most weekdays, especially on Sunday.

Operational implication: focus promotions or bundle offers on weekend periods to narrow the weekday-weekend gap.

### 3) Monthly Revenue Trend

- Clear monthly volatility is present.
- The strongest month is **March**, with another high point in **October**.
- The weakest month is **April**.
- Mid-year months are moderate, followed by recovery into September-October.
- Revenue softens again after October toward year-end.

Operational implication: plan inventory and labor with seasonal ramp-up before expected peak months, and run retention campaigns ahead of lower months.

### 4) Product Performance (Volume vs Revenue)

Top products by quantity:

1. Americano with Milk
2. Latte
3. Americano
4. Cappuccino

Top products by revenue:

1. Latte
2. Americano with Milk
3. Cappuccino
4. Americano

Lower-tier products in both charts are Cocoa, Hot Chocolate, Cortado, and especially Espresso (lowest overall).

Insight: **Latte monetizes best** (top revenue despite being second in volume), while **Americano with Milk drives strongest unit demand**.

### 5) Time-of-Day Product Pattern (Revenue Heatmap)

- **Latte** is dominant across all time buckets and especially strong in Afternoon and Night.
- **Americano with Milk** is very strong in Morning and remains high throughout the day.
- **Cappuccino** is consistently strong, with a noticeable lift at Night.
- **Hot Chocolate** is relatively stronger at Night than Morning/Afternoon.
- **Espresso** remains the weakest contributor across all time buckets.

Operational implication: keep Latte and Americano with Milk as anchor products in all dayparts, and position Hot Chocolate for evening/night-focused campaigns.

### 6) Hour vs Product Transaction Heatmap

- The most intense single cell appears around **10:00 for Americano with Milk**, confirming the late-morning demand surge.
- Latte shows broad strength across many hours, including late afternoon and evening.
- Core demand concentration is around **08:00-17:00**, with a taper at 20:00-22:00.
- Product demand is not uniform by hour, indicating useful opportunities for time-targeted offers.

## Business Recommendations

1. Schedule peak staffing for 09:00-11:00 and 15:00-17:00.
2. Protect stock availability for Latte and Americano with Milk throughout the day.
3. Use weekend and low-hour promotions to stimulate weaker demand windows.
4. Run month-specific campaigns before historically weaker months (especially around April-like dips).
5. Build daypart bundles, for example Morning milk-based coffee offers and Night Hot Chocolate pairings.

## Final Takeaway

Sales performance is driven by a strong daytime rhythm, clear weekday effects, and a product mix led by Latte and Americano with Milk. The biggest practical lever is to align staffing, inventory, and promotions with these recurring time-and-product patterns.
